The Real Issue

Turnover Is Not a
Compensation Problem.

The trucking industry has told itself the same story for forty years: raise pay, reduce turnover. Pay went up. Turnover didn't move. Not in any meaningful, sustained way.

Above the market floor, you are not losing drivers to money. You are losing them to how they feel when they hang up the phone. And almost nobody has ever trained for that.

Dispatchers slowly push out borderline drivers through untrained interactions, inconsistent standards, and communication that transmits the wrong thing under pressure. The first three seconds of an answered call send an entire relationship message.

"Every answered call is either retention — or erosion. There is no neutral option."

About Randy

This Isn't Theory.
It's Proof.

75% → 38%
Turnover reduced in year one. Sustained at top-tier for three consecutive years. No pay increase. No new equipment. One changed variable: how dispatch communicated with drivers.

Randy Fisher is a fleet operations leader, keynote speaker, and communication trainer. He built his credibility the hard way — inside an active operation, where results are measured in retention rates and real dollars, not slide decks.

His first real lesson in people came from a Dealing with Difficult People seminar he walked into defensive and walked out of changed. A mentor named Lance Tyson handed him an uncomfortable truth: the only person you can change is yourself. If you change, everything changes. Randy has spent every year since proving that true — in transportation, where the stakes are real and the margin for error is zero.

He trained nationally for SkillPath Seminars. He was a Dale Carnegie trainer. Then he came into trucking — and discovered that everything he'd learned about human behavior applied directly to the most preventable problem in the industry: drivers who leave because of how they were treated, not because of what they were paid.
